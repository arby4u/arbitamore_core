Privacy Notice & DPIA
GDPR Art. 13
Version 1.0 · Effective 1 January 2025 EU GDPR 2016/679
1. Who We Are

ArbitAmore is the Data Controller for all personal data processed through this application. We are registered in Italy and operate under EU law. Contact: privacy@arbitamore.com

2. What Data We Process
Voice biometric data — your enrolled voiceprint, stored locally on your device only. This is special category data under Article 9 GDPR.
Interaction recordings — timestamped audio of conversations you initiate, involving only consented voices.
Journal entries — text and slider data you enter manually. Stored locally.
Relationship metadata — names or nicknames you assign, relationship type, milestone records.
Anonymised aggregate statistics — see Section 7.
3. Legal Basis (Art. 6 & Art. 9 GDPR)
Consent (Art. 6(1)(a) & Art. 9(2)(a)) — the primary legal basis for all processing. Your consent is explicit, specific, freely given, and recorded with an eIDAS-compliant timestamp at the moment of enrolment. You may withdraw at any time without detriment.
Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f)) — applies only to the retention of anonymised aggregate statistics after individual data deletion (see Section 7). A balancing test confirms this interest does not override your rights.
Third-party voices — any person whose voice is recorded must provide separate, independent consent through the invitation flow before any recording occurs. Non-consenting voices are discarded in real time and never stored.
4. Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)
ArbitAmore processes voice biometric data in a domestic and family law context. This constitutes high-risk processing under Article 35 GDPR, for which a full Data Protection Impact Assessment has been completed.
The DPIA identifies all relevant risks associated with this processing and documents the technical and organisational measures in place to mitigate them. It has been prepared in accordance with the guidelines of the European Data Protection Board (EDPB).
The full DPIA is available to competent supervisory authorities upon request and to data subjects who wish to understand the basis on which their data is processed.
To request a copy of the DPIA or related documentation, contact: privacy@arbitamore.com with the subject line "DPIA Request." We will respond within 30 days.
5. AI Systems Disclosure (EU AI Act)
Arby, our AI assistant, performs voice pattern analysis and factual retrieval from your personal archive. Under the EU AI Act, this system is classified as high-risk due to its use in personal and potentially legal contexts. Accordingly:

✓ Arby does not make autonomous decisions — all outputs are presented to you for review.
✓ Every Arby response is explainable and tied to a timestamped source record.
✓ You may dispute or contextualise any Arby output via the Journal or the Add Context function.
✓ Human oversight is maintained at all times. ArbitAmore takes no legal position on any output.
6. How Long We Keep Your Data
Voice biometric data — retained until you withdraw consent or delete your data. Deletion is immediate and irreversible.
Interaction recordings — retained for as long as your account is active, or until you delete them individually or request full data deletion.
Journal entries & milestones — retained until deleted by you.
Exported records — once exported, copies are outside ArbitAmore systems and subject to the receiving party's own data practices.
7. Anonymised Aggregate Statistics
When you delete your personal data, all identifiable records are permanently erased. However, your interactions will already have contributed to anonymised aggregate statistics — for example, overall harmony/hostility distribution scores across the platform.

These aggregate figures are retained after deletion under the legitimate interests basis (Art. 6(1)(f)) because:

✓ They contain no information that identifies you — no name, voice, timestamp, or individual record.
✓ They cannot be reverse-engineered to reconstruct your personal data.
✓ They serve a genuine public interest: enabling pattern research in coercive control dynamics.
✓ A balancing test has confirmed this interest does not override your individual rights.
✓ If submitted in legal proceedings, aggregate statistics appear in branded ArbitAmore format, bearing no individual attribution, and are presented as population-level data only — not as evidence about any specific person.
8. Your Rights Under GDPR
Right to access (Art. 15) — request a copy of all data we hold about you.
Right to rectification (Art. 16) — correct inaccurate personal data.
Right to erasure (Art. 17) — request deletion of your personal data. Note: anonymised aggregate contributions cannot be deleted as they are no longer personal data.
Right to portability (Art. 20) — export your data in machine-readable format.
Right to withdraw consent — at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.
Right to object (Art. 21) — object to processing based on legitimate interests.
Right to lodge a complaint — with your national Data Protection Authority. In Italy: Garante per la protezione dei dati personali — www.garanteprivacy.it
9. How to Delete Your Data
You may request full deletion of your personal data by visiting:
arbitamore.com/delete-my-data
You will be asked to verify your identity and confirm your request. Deletion is processed within 30 days. You will receive written confirmation by email.

In-app deletion is also available below, after reading this notice in full.
If you prefer to submit your request in writing, email privacy@arbitamore.com with the subject line "Right to Erasure Request."
